How

Getting to Squaw

Squaw is just 42 miles from Reno, 96 miles from Sacramento and 196 miles from San Francisco via all-weather I-80. The valley entrance (look for the Olympic Torch) is just 8 miles south from Truckee and 6 miles north from Tahoe City and the North Shore of Lake Tahoe, on Hwy 89.

For more travel information and details on airport shuttle service to squaw please visit: http://www.squaw.com/trip-planning/getting-here.

Things to do in Squaw

Squaw Valley is an outdoor enthusiasts dream. Home to the 1960 Winter Olympics, Squaw Valley boasts 4,000 acres of skiable terrain and loads of other outdoor activities. If winter sports don't excite you, don't worry, there are loads of other was to entertain yourself in Squaw:

  • Skiing - group lift discount available for Squaw Valley / Alpine Meadows through our wedding coordinator please call 530-xxx-xxxx to book your ticket. Ski and Snowboard rentals are available in Squaw Village as well.
  • Snowshoeing
  • Dog Sledding
  • Snow Tubing
  • Spa Options
    • Resort at Squaw Creek
    • Trilogy Spa in the Village at Squaw Valley
  • Shopping

Things to do in Lake Tahoe

If this is your first trip to Tahoe or your 100th we recommend heading down to the lake to see the awe-inspiring views that Lake Tahoe has to offer. Some of our favorite places include Kings Beach for its laid-back vibe, Historic Truckee for its charm and unique shops, and Sunnyside Lodge for its breathtaking views of the Lake and amazing martinis.

For more information on Lake Tahoe please visit - http://www.visitinglaketahoe.com/
